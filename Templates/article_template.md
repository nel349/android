# Article Template

## Standard Structure

### Hook/Opening
[How you typically start articles]
Engage the reader with a compelling question, a surprising fact, or a brief anecdote related to the topic.

### Problem Statement
[How you frame the problem or topic]
Clearly define the issue or subject matter that the article will address, highlighting its relevance to the target audience.

### Main Content
[Your typical structure: 3 points? Deep dive? Tutorial format?]
- Introduction to the concept or technology.
- Step-by-step explanation or breakdown of the topic.
- Real-world examples or case studies to illustrate key points.

### Examples/Code
[How you present technical examples]
- Use clear, well-commented code snippets.
- Provide context for each example to explain its relevance.
- Highlight key parts of the code to focus the reader's attention.

### Conclusion/Call to Action
[How you wrap up and engage readers]
- Summarize the key takeaways.
- Encourage readers to apply what they've learned.
- Invite feedback, questions, or discussion.

## Article Types
[Different structures for various article types]

### Technical Tutorial
[Structure for step-by-step guides]
- Introduction to the tool/technology.
- Prerequisites and setup instructions.
- Step-by-step walkthrough with code examples.
- Troubleshooting tips and common pitfalls.

### Thought Leadership
[Structure for opinion/analysis pieces]
- Introduction to the topic and its significance.
- Presentation of your viewpoint with supporting arguments.
- Discussion of counterarguments and your rebuttals.
- Conclusion with a call to action for readers to reflect or engage.

### Project Showcase
[Structure for demonstrating work]
- Overview of the project and its goals.
- Description of the development process and challenges faced.
- Key features and functionalities with code snippets.
- Lessons learned and future directions.

## Common Elements
[Recurring sections, formatting preferences, etc.]
- Use of headings and subheadings for clarity.
- Bullet points and numbered lists for easy reading.
- Consistent use of preferred terminology from the style guide.
- Inclusion of diagrams or screenshots where applicable.
